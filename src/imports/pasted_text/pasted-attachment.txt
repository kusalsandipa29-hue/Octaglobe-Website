Based on your R&D direction, brand foundation, logo system, and design philosophy, I would not build OctaGlobe like a software agency website or even a startup website.

I would build it as the public interface of a product operating system.

The visitor should leave remembering **how OctaGlobe thinks**, not simply what it builds.

---

# Color System

Strictly follow the brand system. 

* **Structure:** `#141228` — primary text, structural elements, symbol color.
* **Signal:** `#03EF62` — interaction states, active paths, highlights, progress, and primary actions.
* **Clarity:** `#FFFFFF` — surfaces and separation.

Signal Green should communicate activation, never decoration.

---

# Logo System

Use the prescribed identity consistently. 

* **Wordmark:** Outfit Semi Bold with geometric construction.
* **Symbol:** Fixed eight-point radial mark representing the OctaGlobe core engine and eight core values.
* Preserve the fixed color behavior and avoid decorative treatments.

---

# Interactive Signature

The defining interaction of the site should be an **Operating System Explorer** rather than a conventional homepage.

As the visitor scrolls, the interface gradually constructs a living map of OctaGlobe:

* New nodes appear only after prerequisite concepts are understood.
* Connections animate to explain dependencies rather than attract attention.
* Clicking any module opens its complete lifecycle: **Opportunity → Research → Validation → MVP → Growth → Evolution**.
* Every previously viewed concept remains visible, reinforcing that knowledge accumulates into a coherent operating model.

By the final section, visitors are no longer looking at a collection of pages. They are navigating the complete OctaGlobe operating system—the company's philosophy made tangible. This directly reflects the brand definition of building connected, modular product systems through repeatable research and evolution rather than isolated software projects.
